Sunlight looks white, but it’s actually made up of all the colors of the rainbow. When this light reaches Earth, it has to pass through our atmosphere, which is filled with tiny gas molecules and dust. As the light travels through, it bumps into these particles and gets scattered in all directions. This scattering is what gives the sky its color, and it happens constantly during the day.

The reason the sky looks blue comes down to how different colors behave when they hit air molecules. Blue light has shorter, tighter waves, while red light has longer, wider waves. When sunlight hits the atmosphere, the blue waves get bounced around much more than the other colors. This scattered blue light spreads across the entire sky, so when you look up, your eyes catch mostly this scattered blue light instead of the direct sunlight.

This effect is why the sky stays blue during the day, but it also explains why sunsets and sunrises look red or orange. When the sun is low on the horizon, its light has to travel through much more atmosphere to reach your eyes. By that point, most of the blue light has already been scattered away, leaving the longer red and orange waves to pass through directly. So, the blue sky is just a simple result of how sunlight interacts with the air around us.
