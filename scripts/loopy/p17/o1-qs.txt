   *Introduction:* Quicksort is a highly efficient, divide-and-conquer sorting algorithm. It works by selecting a "pivot" element, partitioning the array around it, and recursively sorting the resulting sub-arrays.

   *Step 1: Choose a Pivot*
   - The pivot can be chosen in various ways: first element, last element, middle, random, or median-of-three.
   - The choice affects performance. Random/middle is often best to avoid worst-case on sorted data.

   *Step 2: Partitioning*
   - Rearrange elements so that all elements less than the pivot come before it, and all elements greater than (or equal to) the pivot come after it.
   - This is typically done in-place using two pointers (i and j) or a similar approach.
   - After partitioning, the pivot is in its final sorted position.

   *Step 3: Recursive Sorting*
   - Apply quicksort recursively to the left sub-array (elements < pivot) and the right sub-array (elements > pivot).
   - The base case is when a sub-array has 0 or 1 elements, which is already sorted.
