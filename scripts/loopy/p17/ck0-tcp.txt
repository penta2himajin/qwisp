    *   *Introduction:* TCP is a reliable protocol. If the network is congested, packets drop. TCP needs to detect this and reduce the sending rate. It uses a variable called the **Congestion Window (CWND)**.

    *   *Slow Start:*
        *   *Concept:* When a connection starts, TCP doesn't know the capacity. It starts small.
        *   *Mechanism:* It starts with a CWND of 1 segment (or 10 bytes in modern implementations).
        *   *Growth:* Every time an ACK is received, the CWND increases by 1 segment. This is *exponential* growth (1, 2, 4, 8, 16...).
        *   *The "Slow Start" Threshold (SSThresh):* This is the limit. Once CWND reaches SSThresh, it stops exponential growth and switches to Congestion Avoidance.

    *   *Congestion Avoidance:*
        *   *Concept:* Once we know the capacity (reached SSThresh), we want to be careful not to overshoot.
        *   *Mechanism:* Linear growth. For every ACK received, CWND increases by 1 segment (or 1 MSS).
        *   *Why:* It prevents the "burst" behavior of Slow Start. It probes the network slowly.
